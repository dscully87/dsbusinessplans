Executive summary

A personalization-first learning engine that adapts to a child's developmental stage.

miStuff is a privacy-first educational platform that turns a child's own surroundings into a dynamic learning environment. Our flagship product, a Progressive Web App, uses a user-generated library of photos to build a personalized curriculum that grows with the child. While we use the early-years developmental stage (0 to 4) as our primary beachhead market, the platform architecture is designed to support evolving developmental needs, including language acquisition, speech therapy, and hobby-based learning for children beyond the age of four.

We are built in accordance with GDPR-K and global children's safety standards. With the core platform already built and shipping, we are seeking a partnership with the Local Enterprise Office (LEO) to fund an R&D and market-testing programme that will take miStuff from a successful beachhead product to a scalable, internationally traded service.

03

The origin story

As a parent, I identified the real-world gap: children often cannot transfer skills learned from generic cartoon apples to the actual apple in their home. My daughter is the primary user and first-wave tester, keeping the product grounded in immediate pedagogical relevance.

The problem

A trust crisis around "generic" content in the children's app category.

No real-world transfer

Current apps use stock, generic media. A child who learns a stock dog often fails to generalise that word to their own family pet.

The privacy trade-off

Most popular apps monetize attention through ads or data. No major competitor offers a premium, ad-free experience that uses the family's own library as the learning engine.

One-size-fits-none

Fixed English word banks exclude multilingual families, children with speech delays, and neurodivergent learners who need a more flexible, personalized curriculum.

The solution

An offline-first personalization engine, with games as a swappable content layer.

The core product is the photo and object engine: the infrastructure that captures a family's belongings, curates them into collections, and turns them into structured learning content. The games themselves sit on top of that engine as a content layer, one that can be swapped out or extended as the child's developmental needs change, without rebuilding the underlying platform.

01

Capture and curate

Caregivers photograph objects, group them into collections and teach the app what the child recognises.

02

Play and learn

The engine auto-generates games (naming, matching, identifying) from the family's own photos, the first of several content layers built on the same infrastructure.

03

Privacy by design

Data isolation is built into the architecture. We sell no ads, use no third-party trackers and hold a strict 90-day photo retention policy. We comply with the UK Age-Appropriate Design Code and GDPR-K, positioning us to enter the European and international markets on a safety moat.

Life-stage scalability Unlike fixed-content apps that become obsolete as a child ages, miStuff evolves. Because the engine, not any single game, is the core product, each new developmental stage is a new content layer on existing infrastructure rather than a new product.

0 to 4 years: the beachhead

Object recognition, vocabulary building, and caregiver engagement. Our initial wedge and near-term revenue base.

4 to 7 years: language and logic

Complex categorization, reading support, and second-language acquisition. Because the content is the family's own photos, it gives a child immediate, personal context for learning new words in a target language.

Specialized needs: speech and language therapy

The ability to curate specific sets of objects lets therapists build custom exercises that generic stock-photo apps cannot replicate, opening a clinician-led B2B2C channel.

Go-to-market and growth strategy

Lead with the demoable moment, then convert through trusted channels.

The demoable moment

  • Short-form video (TikTok, Reels) showing a child reacting to a photo of their own toy. It is inherently shareable and demonstrates the value proposition in seconds.

B2B2C expansion

  • Once consumer retention is proven, we will use the Clinic Mode feature to sell to speech and language pathologists, a warm referral loop in which clinicians recommend miStuff to parents, acting as a low-CAC acquisition channel.

Globalisation

  • Because our content is user-generated photos, the app is language-agnostic. It can launch in any market (Germany, France, Spain) with zero content-licensing costs.
Competitive landscape and market size

A large market, and a moat incumbents cannot easily copy.

Total addressable market

The global children's educational app market is valued in the billions, with double-digit annual growth rates.

The moat

Incumbents such as Duolingo ABC and Khan Academy Kids have massive reach but cannot replicate our personalisation moat. Every photo a parent adds increases the app's switching cost.

GDPR advantage

As an Irish-domiciled entity with a compliant architecture, we are protected from the regulatory volatility facing US-based tech competitors who struggle to meet EU privacy standards.

Risk assessment and mitigation

Where this gets hard, and the plan for it.

CAC versus LTV Key risk

Customer acquisition cost could outpace lifetime value. Mitigation: a lean-launch strategy, running small, budget-capped ad tests in the UK and Ireland markets to prove that LTV supports CAC before scaling marketing spend.

Content cold start Key risk

Personalisation requires the parent to add photos before the app feels useful—the "empty app" problem. Mitigation: a Starter Pack, a pre-loaded library of common objects, gets the parent to the "play" moment in under 60 seconds even before they upload their own photos.

Regulatory compliance Moderate

Children's-privacy law and app-store rules are strict and shifting. Mitigation: my background in HealthTech quality management systems ensures our compliance documentation (DPIA, breach runbooks, parental gate architecture) is audit-ready from day one.

Conclusion

A low-risk, high-reward opportunity to back an Irish tech startup with genuine global potential.

miStuff is not just an app; it is a privacy-first learning infrastructure. By combining a mature, already-built product with a founder who has deep expertise in regulated software, we offer the LEO a low-risk, high-reward opportunity to support an Irish tech start-up with genuine global potential.
